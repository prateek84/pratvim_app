Buttons and actions

All clickable controls need normal, hover, active/pressed, keyboard focus, loading, and disabled treatment. Hover applies to pointer-based websites; mobile app controls use pressed feedback instead.

Forms

Labels remain visible above fields. Inputs use 46-48px height on the web and 48-56px in the app. Validation appears near the field and must not depend on color alone.

Accessibility and content

Safety-oriented products need especially clear language, predictable controls, and visible state changes. Every interaction should work without color, hover, or animation.

Do
  • Maintain at least 4.5:1 text contrast and 3:1 for large text and essential UI boundaries.
  • Use 44 x 44px minimum touch targets and visible keyboard focus.
  • Use labels that remain visible after typing.
  • Announce loading, success, errors, and modal changes to assistive technology.
  • Respect reduced-motion preferences and keep layouts stable while loading.
  • Write calm, direct safety messages with a constructive next action.
Do not
  • Use muted gray for essential instructions or errors.
  • Communicate state with color alone.
  • Disable actions without explaining what is required.
  • Use all caps for sentences or long labels.
  • Place cards inside cards or use excessive shadows.
  • Use playful wording for serious safety, privacy, or account errors.
